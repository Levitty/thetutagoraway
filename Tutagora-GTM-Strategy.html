Executive Summary

Tutagora is a Kenyan ed-tech platform that connects students with verified tutors for live, one-on-one lessons via video, collaborative whiteboard, and an AI-powered Math Tutor. The platform currently serves 4 school partners with 100% retention.

This go-to-market strategy outlines how Tutagora will acquire its first 500 students, 50 tutors, and 20 school partners within 12 months. It addresses all three sides of the marketplace simultaneously: students/parents who create demand, tutors who provide supply, and schools that act as trusted distribution channels.

The Opportunity: Kenya's online learning market is projected to reach $174.8 million by 2029 (20.66% growth). While 80% of Kenyan parents know about online tutoring, only 9% have tried it — a massive awareness-to-adoption gap that Tutagora can exploit.
The Edge: Unlike competitors (Eneza, Zeraki, Kytabu), Tutagora offers live 1-on-1 tutoring with verified teachers plus an AI tutor for self-paced practice. This combination of human expertise and AI support doesn't exist in the Kenyan market.

Market Landscape

The Kenyan Ed-Tech Environment

Kenya is one of Africa's three leading ed-tech markets alongside Nigeria and South Africa. Over 234 ed-tech startups operate in Kenya, but the tutoring sub-segment remains underserved. The government's new Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) makes digital literacy a core skill from early grades, creating institutional demand for technology-enhanced learning.

Target Audiences

1. Parents & Students

Primary: Urban middle-class parents in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, and Nakuru with children in Grades 4–12 and Form 1–4.
Secondary: University students preparing for professional exams (CPA, ACCA).
Pain Points: Difficulty finding reliable, verified tutors; inflexible scheduling; no progress tracking.
Key Message: "Verified tutors, flexible scheduling, real results — all from home."

2. Tutors

Primary: University graduates and working professionals with teaching qualifications who want flexible, supplementary income.
Secondary: Retired teachers and full-time private tutors looking for a platform to find students.
Pain Points: Finding students is hard; collecting payment is unreliable; no digital teaching tools.
Key Message: "Teach from anywhere, get paid weekly via M-Pesa, we handle everything else."

3. Schools & Institutions

Primary: Private primary and secondary schools in urban Kenya that want to offer tutoring as a value-add.
Secondary: International schools and school groups looking for after-school enrichment.
Pain Points: Parents demand supplementary support but schools can't staff it; no technology to manage tutoring at scale.
Key Message: "Give your parents the tutoring support they're asking for — branded as your school's program."

Go-To-Market Strategy

Tutagora will execute a three-engine approach, running all three simultaneously but with phased intensity.

1 School Partnerships (Months 1–3 Focus)

Schools are the fastest path to trusted distribution. Each school partnership gives access to hundreds of parents who already trust the school's recommendation.

Playbook

  1. Identify 30 target private schools in Nairobi and Mombasa. Prioritize schools with 200+ students, active parent WhatsApp groups, and a principal who is open to innovation.
  2. Cold outreach via email and LinkedIn to school principals and academic directors. Lead with the free AI Math Tutor as a no-risk trial.
  3. In-person demo at the school (30 minutes). Show the AI Tutor, live lesson with whiteboard, and admin dashboard. Leave a one-pager and QR code for teachers to try.
  4. Pilot program: offer 20 free AI Tutor seats for one month. Track usage and share progress reports with the school.
  5. Convert to paid: after pilot, present results to parents at a school assembly or parent meeting. Offer a school-branded tutoring package.

2 Direct-to-Parent Acquisition (Months 2–6 Focus)

While schools provide trust, direct acquisition builds the brand and captures parents who don't want to wait for their school to offer the service.

A. WhatsApp Marketing (Primary Channel)

WhatsApp is the most-used messaging platform in Kenya. Parents are already in school WhatsApp groups and community groups.

  • Create a "Tutagora Parents" broadcast list. Share weekly study tips, free AI Tutor invites, and success stories.
  • Partner with parent community admins in Nairobi estates (Kilimani, Lavington, South B, South C, Rongai, Kitengela) to share Tutagora in their groups.
  • Run a "First Lesson Free" referral campaign: every parent who shares gets KSh 500 off when their referral books.

B. Facebook & Instagram

Facebook is the dominant social platform for Kenyan parents. Instagram targets younger, aspirational parents.

  • Run targeted ads: "Your child can have a verified tutor for KSh 1,000/hour" targeting parents aged 28–50 in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu.
  • Post 3x/week: tutor spotlights, student success stories, quick math tips from the AI tutor, behind-the-scenes of lesson prep.
  • Join and contribute to parenting Facebook groups (Nairobi Mums, Kenyan Parents Forum, etc.) with helpful advice — not hard sells.

C. TikTok & YouTube Shorts

Short-form video drives awareness among younger parents and students directly.

  • Create 30–60 second videos: "Watch the AI Tutor solve this KCSE question", tutor tips, student testimonials.
  • Recruit 2–3 tutor-influencers to post weekly educational content tagged #Tutagora.

D. Google SEO & Ads

  • Target keywords: "online tutor Kenya", "maths tutor Nairobi", "KCSE tutor", "private tutor Kenya".
  • Publish SEO blog posts: "How to Choose a Tutor in Kenya", "KCSE Maths Tips", "CBC Grade 5 Math Practice".
  • Run Google Ads on high-intent searches with landing pages tailored to each subject.

3 Tutor Recruitment (Months 1–4 Focus)

A marketplace fails without supply. Tutor recruitment runs in parallel with everything else.

Recruitment Channels

  1. University career boards: Post on UoN, KU, Strathmore, JKUAT, and Moi University job boards. "Earn KSh 850/hour teaching what you studied."
  2. LinkedIn outreach: Target education graduates and working professionals with teaching backgrounds.
  3. Teacher WhatsApp groups: Kenya has active teacher communities on WhatsApp. Post opportunities there.
  4. Referral bonus: KSh 1,000 to any tutor who refers another verified tutor.
  5. School teacher moonlighting: Approach private school teachers who want extra income after school hours.

Pricing Strategy

Tutagora uses a marketplace model: students pay per lesson, Tutagora takes a 15% platform fee, and tutors receive 85%. Payments are processed via Paystack (1.5% transaction fee on M-Pesa, card payments supported via Visa/Mastercard).
