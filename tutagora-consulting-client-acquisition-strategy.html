01 — Strategic Positioning

Own a Category, Not a Skill

Right now, "Tutagora Consulting" competes with every freelancer and agency in Kenya offering marketing or web development. That is a losing game. The path to faster client acquisition is narrowing your positioning to own a specific intersection that nobody else owns.

Your Unique Position

"We are the only consulting firm in Kenya that combines school marketing, school management software, and AI-powered learning — all under one roof."

This is not a tagline. It is a positioning statement that should drive every piece of content, every pitch, and every outreach message.

Two Lanes of Positioning

Lane 1: Education Specialist

For schools, universities, and education institutions. Lead with deep domain expertise — you understand term cycles, parent decision journeys, KICD requirements, and school administration pain.

Primary Lane

Lane 2: Growth Partner for SMEs

For startups and mid-market businesses who need marketing + tech but cannot afford separate agencies for each. Lead with the "one partner" value proposition — brand, website, social, and custom software.

Secondary Lane

Messaging Framework

Every piece of content and outreach should hit one of these three pillars:

  • Pillar 1 — Credibility: "We have worked with 4 schools, maintained 100% client retention, and delivered across 6+ service areas." Show proof, case studies, numbers.
  • Pillar 2 — Integration: "Most schools hire one agency for marketing, another for software, and try to make them talk. We do both under one roof — so your brand and your systems actually align."
  • Pillar 3 — Local Expertise: "We are based in Nairobi and understand the Kenyan market. We build for Kenyan realities — M-Pesa integration, local SEO, Swahili content, and KICD-aligned curricula."

03 — The Acquisition Funnel

From Stranger to Signed Client

Every prospect goes through these stages. Your job is to build systems for each stage so leads move through automatically, with you only stepping in for high-value conversations.

Stage 1 — Awareness

They discover you exist

Channels: Google search, LinkedIn content, referrals, Facebook groups, events, paid ads. Goal: get them to your website or landing page.

Stage 2 — Interest

They engage with your content

Actions: read a blog post, watch a case study video, download a free resource (e.g. "10-Point School Marketing Checklist"). Goal: capture their email.

Stage 3 — Consideration

They evaluate you as a potential partner

Actions: receive your email nurture sequence, see your case studies, visit your consulting page multiple times. Goal: book a discovery call.

Stage 4 — Decision

Discovery call and proposal

Actions: 30-min discovery call (you diagnose their problem), receive a custom proposal within 48 hours. Goal: signed contract.

Stage 5 — Retention & Referral

They become a client who refers others

Actions: monthly check-ins, quarterly reviews, referral incentive programme. Goal: 100% retention + 2 referrals per client per year.

04 — Channel Playbooks

Where and How to Get Discovered

A. Google / SEO (Highest ROI Long-term)

When a school director searches "school marketing agency Kenya" or an SME founder searches "web developer Nairobi", you need to be on page one. SEO is the highest-ROI channel for consulting firms because the intent is already there.

Target Keywords (High Intent)

Education: school marketing Kenya, school management software, school website design Nairobi, digital marketing for schools, school branding agency

SME/General: marketing agency Nairobi, web development company Kenya, social media marketing Kenya, branding agency Kenya, custom software Kenya

Long-tail: how to market a school in Kenya, school enrollment marketing strategies, best school management system Kenya, affordable web design Nairobi

SEO Action Plan

  • Google Business Profile: Create and optimize immediately. Add photos, services, and ask your 4 current clients for Google reviews. This alone puts you on the map for local searches.
  • Blog content: Publish 2 articles per month targeting long-tail keywords. Each article should be 1,500+ words, practical, and include a call to action for a discovery call.
  • Landing pages: Create dedicated pages for each service (e.g. /school-marketing, /school-software, /web-design-nairobi). Each page targets specific keywords and has a clear CTA.
  • Technical SEO: Your Vercel-hosted site is fast. Add meta titles, descriptions, schema markup (LocalBusiness), and an XML sitemap.

B. LinkedIn (Best for B2B & Corporates)

LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm rewards personal profiles over company pages (65% vs 5% of feed allocation). This means your personal LinkedIn profile is more important than the Tutagora company page.

LinkedIn Playbook

  • Profile optimization: Your headline should be "I help schools and businesses grow through marketing + technology | Founder, Tutagora Consulting". Not just "CEO at Tutagora".
  • Post 3-4 times per week: Share frameworks, case study snippets, behind-the-scenes of client work (with permission), and opinions on Kenyan business/education. Carousel posts get 2-3x more engagement than text.
  • Comment on 10 posts daily: Find school directors, education leaders, and SME founders. Leave thoughtful, value-adding comments. This puts you on their radar organically.
  • Connect with intent: Send 20-30 connection requests per week to people who match your ideal client profiles. Use a personalized note (not a sales pitch).
  • LinkedIn newsletters: Start a monthly newsletter on LinkedIn on education marketing or business growth in Kenya. Subscribers get notified of each issue.

C. Facebook & Instagram (Best for Schools)

School directors and parents are more active on Facebook than LinkedIn. Join and contribute to groups like "Private School Owners Kenya", "KEPSHA", and county-level education groups.

Facebook Playbook

  • Join 5-10 relevant groups: Do not spam. Provide genuine advice 3-5 times per week. When someone asks "how do I market my school", give a thorough answer. Position yourself as the go-to expert.
  • Run targeted ads (when budget allows): $100-200/month on Facebook ads targeting school administrators within Kenya. Ad leads to a free downloadable guide (e.g. "The School Marketing Playbook").
  • Instagram portfolio: Post before/after design work, student success stories from partner schools, and short video walkthroughs of your software. Use location tags (Nairobi, Kenya).

D. Events & Networking (Highest Conversion Rate)

Face-to-face sells consulting faster than any digital channel. A 15-minute conversation at an event can do what 20 emails cannot.

Event Playbook

  • Attend 2 events per month: KEPSHA conferences, Kenya Association of Manufacturers networking, Nairobi tech meetups, school administrators forums.
  • Speak at events: Offer to give a free 20-minute talk on "How Schools Can Use Digital Marketing to Boost Enrollment" or "5 Tech Tools Every Kenyan Business Needs". Event organizers are always looking for speakers.
  • Host your own workshops: Quarterly half-day workshop for school directors — "The Digital School Blueprint". Free or minimal fee. This positions you as an authority and fills your pipeline.
  • Follow up within 24 hours: Every business card becomes a LinkedIn connection + a personalized follow-up email within one day. Automate the follow-up sequence.

E. Google Ads (Quick Wins)

When budget allows, run Google Search ads on high-intent keywords. Someone searching "school management software Kenya" is ready to buy — meet them there.

Google Ads Budget Guide

Start with $150-300/month. Target 5-10 high-intent keywords with exact match. Send traffic to dedicated landing pages (not your homepage). Expected cost per lead: $5-15 for Kenya. Expected leads: 10-30/month at this budget.

Make Leads Nurture Themselves

This is where you scale without adding staff. Every repetitive action in your sales process can be automated — from lead capture to follow-up to proposal delivery.

System 1: Website Lead Capture to Email Sequence

When someone fills out your consulting page contact form, the lead should automatically enter a nurture sequence.

→

→

"Book a call" email (Day 7)

Email Sequence Details

  • Email 1 (Immediate): Thank them for reaching out. Share a brief overview of what Tutagora does. Include a link to your best case study. Sign off with "I will be in touch within 24 hours."
  • Email 2 (Day 2): Share a case study relevant to their industry. "Here is how we helped Whitestar Group of Schools increase enrollment by building a complete digital presence." Include before/after results.
  • Email 3 (Day 5): Provide pure value — a free tip or insight related to their industry. No selling. "3 things every school website needs to convert visitors into applicants."
  • Email 4 (Day 7): Soft CTA — "Would you like to schedule a 30-minute discovery call to see if we can help? Here is my calendar link."
  • Email 5 (Day 14): Final nudge — "Just checking in. If now is not the right time, no problem. I will share some useful resources with you from time to time."

06 — Content Engine

Become the Authority Before They Need You

Content is how you build trust at scale. When a school director googles a problem and finds YOUR article with a clear answer, you become the obvious choice when they are ready to hire.

Content Calendar (Monthly Template)

Week 1: Blog Post

Long-form article (1,500+ words) on your blog targeting a specific keyword. Example: "The Complete Guide to Marketing Your School in Kenya (2026)"

SEO

Week 2: Case Study

Detailed breakdown of work with a client. Problem, approach, results. With screenshots and real numbers. Share on LinkedIn + website.

Trust

Week 3: Free Resource

Downloadable PDF guide, checklist, or template. Gate it behind an email form. Example: "School Digital Marketing Checklist (Free PDF)"

Lead Capture

Week 4: Video/Carousel

Short video tip (1-2 min) or LinkedIn carousel breaking down a concept. Carousels get 2-3x more engagement than text posts on LinkedIn.

Engagement

LinkedIn Content Themes (3-4 posts per week)

  • Monday — Insight post: Share a framework, mental model, or contrarian opinion about marketing or education in Kenya. "Most schools waste money on marketing because they skip this one step..."
  • Wednesday — Story post: Share a behind-the-scenes story from a project. "Last month, a school came to us with declining enrollment. Here is what we found..." Personal stories get highest engagement.
  • Friday — Value post: Give away a practical tip, tool recommendation, or template. "Here are 5 free tools every Kenyan school should be using for their marketing."
  • Saturday (optional) — Community post: Ask a question, run a poll, or celebrate a win. "What is the biggest marketing challenge for schools in Kenya right now?"

Lead Magnets (Gated Resources)

These are downloadable resources that require an email to access. They are the bridge between awareness and your email nurture sequence.

  • "The 2026 School Marketing Playbook" — 15-page PDF guide covering everything from Google Business setup to social media calendars. Target: school directors.
  • "Website Audit Checklist for Kenyan Businesses" — One-page checklist that any business can use to evaluate their website. Target: SMEs.
  • "5 Signs Your School Needs a Management System" — Short diagnostic document. Target: school administrators using spreadsheets.
  • "Digital Marketing ROI Calculator" — Simple spreadsheet that estimates ROI from marketing investment. Target: budget-conscious decision-makers.
07 — Partnerships & Referrals

Let Other People Sell for You

Referral-driven clients convert at 3-5x the rate of cold leads and cost almost nothing to acquire. Building a structured referral programme is one of the highest-leverage things you can do.

Referral Programme Structure

Client Referrals

Offer existing clients a 10% discount on their next month for every successful referral. Or a flat KSh 10,000 referral bonus. Make it easy — give them a shareable link or a template message they can forward.

Low Cost, High Trust

Strategic Partnerships

Partner with complementary service providers who serve the same clients but do not compete with you. Examples below. Create formal referral agreements with a revenue share (10-15% commission on referred work).

Scalable

Partnership Targets

  • School supply companies: They visit schools regularly and have relationships with directors. Partner with them to recommend Tutagora for marketing and software.
  • Accountants and bookkeepers: SME accountants hear about business challenges first. When a client says "I need a website" or "I need more customers", you want to be who they recommend.
  • Printers and signage companies: Schools and businesses that need print materials often also need digital marketing. Cross-referral arrangement.
  • EdTech companies: Companies selling to schools (e.g. KUCCPS platforms, exam systems) can refer clients who need marketing. You can refer schools to them for complementary tools.
  • Freelance developers: Developers who get marketing requests they cannot handle. Send a 15% referral fee for signed clients.

Testimonial Collection System

After every project milestone, send your client a 3-question email: (1) What was the main challenge before working with us? (2) What results have you seen? (3) Would you recommend us? Turn their answers into a testimonial for your website, LinkedIn, and proposals. Automate this with a recurring calendar reminder.

08 — Outbound Strategy

Go to Them Before They Come to You

While your inbound engine builds over time, outbound gives you immediate conversations. The key is personalization — generic cold emails get deleted. Personalized, value-first outreach gets responses.

Cold Email Sequence (Schools)

Email 1 — The Opener (Day 1)

Subject: Quick thought on [School Name]'s online presence

Hi [Name],

I was looking at [School Name]'s website and noticed a few things that might be costing you enquiries — particularly around mobile experience and Google visibility.

We recently helped Whitestar Group of Schools redesign their digital presence, and they saw a noticeable uptick in parent enquiries within the first term.

Would a 15-minute call to share what we found be useful? Happy to walk you through it — no strings attached.

Best,
Levitty

Email 2 — The Value Add (Day 4)

Subject: Re: Quick thought on [School Name]'s online presence

Hi [Name],

Following up — I put together a quick 3-point breakdown of things [School Name] could improve online (took me 10 minutes, no charge):

1. [Specific observation about their website]
2. [Their Google Business profile status]
3. [Social media presence observation]

If any of these resonate, I would be happy to jump on a call and discuss.

Best,
Levitty

Outbound Volume Target

  • 20-30 personalized emails per week — this means researching each school or business before emailing. Quality over quantity.
  • Build a target list of 200 schools and 100 SMEs — using Google Maps, school directories, and LinkedIn. Research each one and note 1-2 observations about their current digital presence.
  • Expected response rate: 10-15% for personalized outreach. At 25 emails/week, expect 2-4 conversations per week.

10 — 90-Day Launch Plan

What to Do First

Week 1-2

Foundation Setup

  • Create and optimize Google Business Profile with photos, services, hours, and description
  • Set up Brevo account — import existing contacts, create welcome email template
  • Set up Calendly with a "Discovery Call" link (30 minutes)
  • Update your consulting page contact form to connect to Brevo via Zapier
  • Optimize your LinkedIn profile with the positioning headline
  • Ask all 4 current clients for Google reviews and LinkedIn testimonials
  • Write your first case study (Whitestar Group of Schools)
Week 3-4

Content & Outreach Launch

  • Publish first blog post targeting "school marketing Kenya"
  • Create your first lead magnet: "The 2026 School Marketing Playbook" (PDF)
  • Build a landing page in Brevo for the lead magnet
  • Set up the 5-email nurture sequence in Brevo
  • Start posting on LinkedIn 3x/week
  • Build outbound target list: 50 schools + 25 SMEs with contact details
  • Send first batch of 15 personalized cold emails
Week 5-6

Paid Channels & Partnerships

  • Launch Google Ads campaign with 5 high-intent keywords
  • Launch Facebook ad promoting lead magnet to school directors
  • Reach out to 5 potential referral partners (school suppliers, accountants)
  • Join 5 Facebook groups for school owners and start contributing
  • Continue cold email outreach: 20/week
  • Publish second blog post
  • Create and share your first LinkedIn carousel
Week 7-8

Optimize & Scale

  • Review Google Ads performance — pause low-performing keywords, increase bids on winners
  • Review email sequence open/click rates — refine subject lines and CTAs
  • Publish your second case study
  • Attend your first industry event and collect 15+ contacts
  • Follow up all event contacts within 24 hours and add to CRM
  • Formalize referral programme with first 2 partners
Week 9-12

Compound & Convert

  • By now you should have 30-50 leads in your pipeline. Focus on conversion.
  • Aim to close 1-2 new clients from accumulated pipeline
  • Plan and promote your first workshop ("The Digital School Blueprint")
  • Create a second lead magnet for SMEs
  • Publish 2 more blog posts
  • Review all channels — double down on what is working, cut what is not
  • Build your month 4-6 plan based on data

  • Website traffic (Google Analytics)
  • LinkedIn profile views & post impressions
  • Google Business Profile views
  • Social media follower growth

Lead Metrics

  • Leads per month (target: 15-25)

Conversion Metrics

  • Discovery calls booked per month (target: 8-12)
  • Proposal-to-close rate (target: 40%+)
  • Average deal size
  • Time from first contact to signed deal

Revenue Metrics

  • New MRR from consulting
  • Client retention rate (target: 90%+)
  • Referrals per client per year (target: 2)
